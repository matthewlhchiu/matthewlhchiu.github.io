# Welcome, fellow traveller
Thank you for visiting my small corner of the Internet world, where I document my journey so far and share what's to come.*

I am a philosophy, politics and economics student at the [London School of Economics](https://www.lse.ac.uk).

In enjoy writing and thinking about economics, artificial intelligence, cities, and social systems. I occasional pontificate about the world on my [Substack](https://matthewlhchiu.substack.com/).

More generally, this website is a disparate collection of my thoughts, notes, and weakly-held opinions. I hope you find them interesting.

## My work
I am currently working on research in **AI policy and governance**, **global priorities**, and **development economics**. More generally, my interests are wide-ranging and include the economics of innovation, education policy, economic growth, labour allocation, and industrial organisation.

As a student of the social sciences and humanities, I have been blessed with the opportunity to think deeply about many problems. I discuss some of these topics [here]({% link ppe.md %}).

I've recently been reading [*The Meritocracy Trap*](https://www.amazon.co.uk/Meritocracy-Trap-Foundational-Inequality-Dismantles/dp/0735221995) by Daniel Markovits. Reading this book has triggered a lot of thinking about how the systems that that shape us — the meritocratic ideal, the democratic spirit, the educational system, and the idol of prestige — came to be. 

I previously worked in management consulting, venture capital and talent investing, and early-stage startups in the crypto and social impact space.
